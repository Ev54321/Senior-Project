The lacrosse information page

What is lacrosse and what do you need to know before getting into lacrosse?

Start of Game

During the warm-ups, before the game starts the team captains of each team are called to the center by the ref to do a coin flip. The winner of the coin flip will either get to choose which side of the field to start on or get AP (Alternating Posession - Decides which team gets ball after a neutral play stopage.)

At the start of the game each team sends out 10 players. 3 defense, 3 middies, 3 offense, and 1 goalie. They line up in the middle of the field, standing facing across eachother with goalies on opposite ends.

The goalies shake hands in the middle followed by the rest of the team.

The start of each possesion begins with a face-off.

Game Rules

• You can only have 10 players (including the goalie) on the field at a time.

• only six players from each team allowed on one half of the field at a time.

• No intentionally hitting anywhere above the shoulders

• No intentionallly hitting below the waist

• 60 second shot clock

Lacrosse Terms to Know

Crease - The blue circle around the goal where defense can enter but not offense

The Box - A 40x35 yard area marked by a line 20 yards from the goal

GLE - (Goal Line Extended) An imaginarey line parallel to the goal that goes from sideline to sideline
